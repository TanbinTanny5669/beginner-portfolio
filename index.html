Why is the world called a world? The ancient root of world meant 'age or life of man'. The first part is the same as were- in werewolf (see wolf)—it means 'man'—and the second part is related to old. The Anglo-Saxons first used world to mean 'human existence, life on earth' as opposed to future life in heaven or hell.
